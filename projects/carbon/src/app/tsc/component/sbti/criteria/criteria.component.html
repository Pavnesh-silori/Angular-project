Emissions coverage
C7- Scope 1, 2 and 3 allowable exclusions: Companies may exclude up to 5% of scope 1 and scope 2 emissions combined in the boundary of the inventory and target. Companies may exclude a maximum of 5% of emissions from their total scope 3 inventory.
C8- Scope 3 emissions coverage for near-term targets: Companies must set one or more emission reduction near-term targets that collectively cover(s) at least two-thirds (67%) of total reported and excluded scope 3 emissions.

Method validity for near and long term targets
C10- Method validity: Targets must be modeled using the latest version of methods and tools approved by the initiative.
Emissions accounting requirements
C11- Scope 2 accounting approach: Companies shall disclose whether they are using a location- or market-based accounting approach to calculate base year emissions and to track performance against a science-based target.
C12- Scope 3 inventory: Companies must complete a scope 3 inventory covering gross scope 3 emissions for all its emissions sources
Scope 3 inventory is complete and confirmed as base year emissions, view base year emissions
C13- Bioenergy accounting: CO2 emissions from the combustion, processing and distribution phase of bioenergy shall be reported alongside a company's GHG inventory and included in the target boundary when setting a science-based target.
C14- Carbon credits: The use of carbon credits must not be counted as emission reductions toward the progress of companies' near-term or long-term science-based targets.
C15- Avoided emissions: Avoided emissions do not count toward near-term or long-term science-based emission reduction targets.
Netzero definition
C16- State of net-zero emissions: Companies shall set one or more targets to reach a state of netzero emissions, which involves: (a) reducing scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions to zero in eligible 1.5°C scenarios or sector pathways and (b) neutralizing any residual emissions at the net-zero target date - and any GHG emissions released into the atmosphere thereafter.
Structure
C17- Net-zero target structure: Companies aiming to reach a state of net-zero emissions in a timeframe exceeding 10 years shall set both near-term and long-term science-based emission reduction targets. If a company's near-term target meets the ambition requirements of a long-term target, then the latter is not required.
Timeframe
C18- Base year: The base year must be no earlier than 2015. The company shall use the same base year for its long-term science-based targets as its near-term SBTs.
C19- Target year(s): Near-term targets must cover a minimum of 5 years and a maximum of 10 years from the date the target is submitted to the SBTi for validation. Long-term targets shall have a target year no later than 2050
C20- Progress to date: The minimum forward-looking ambition of near-term targets is consistent with reaching net-zero by 2050 at the latest

Ambition
C21- Level of ambition for scope 1 and 2 targets: At a minimum, scope 1 and scope 2 targets must have 1.5C ambition. This applies to both near-term and long-term targets.
C22- Absolute targets: Absolute reduction targets for scope 1 and scope 2 are eligible when 1.5C ambition is met or aligned with the relevant 1.5°C sector-specific absolute pathway (long-term targets only).
C23- Intensity targets: Intensity targets for scope 1 and scope 2 emissions are eligible when they are modeled using an approved 1.5°C sector pathway applicable to companies business activities.
C24- Level of ambition for scope 3 emissions reductions targets: At a minimum, scope 3 targets must have well-below 2C ambition. For long term target, minimum ambition is 1.5C.
C25- Supplier or Customer Engagement Targets: Near-term targets to drive the adoption of science-based emission reduction targets by their suppliers and/or customers are acceptable when the following conditions are met:
  • Boundary: Companies may set engagement targets around relevant and credible upstream or downstream categories.
  • Formulation: Companies shall provide information in the target language on what percentage of emissions from relevant upstream and/or downstream categories is covered by the engagement target or, if that information is not available, what percentage of annual procurement spend is covered by the target.
  • Timeframe: Companies' engagement targets must be fulfilled within a maximum of 5 years from the date the company's target is submitted to the SBTi for validation.
  • Ambition Level: The company's suppliers/customers shall have science-based emission reduction targets in line with the latest version of the SBTi Criteria for Near-term Targets.
C26- Absolute targets (scope 3): Absolute targets for scope 3 are eligible when they are consistent with the well below 2°C goal (near-term targets), the 1.5°C goal (long-term targets), or aligned with the relevant 1.5°C sector-specific absolute pathway (long-term targets only).
C27- Intensity targets (scope 3): Intensity targets for scope 3 are eligible when they are modeled using an approved sector-specific physical intensity pathway where applicable to companies business activities or using eligible physical intensity or economic intensity approaches. This applies to both near-term and long-term targets.
Combined targets (near and long-term targets)
C28- Combined scope targets: Targets combining scopes (e.g., 1 and 2, or 1, 2 and 3) are permitted if the SBTi can review the ambition of the individual target components and confirm each meets the relevant ambition criteria.
Not applicable
Renewable electricity targets (near and long-term targets)
C29- Renewable electricity (scope 2 only): The SBTi has identified 80% renewable electricity procurement by 2025 and 100% by 2030 as thresholds for this approach.
Neutralization
C30 - Neutralization of unabated emissions to reach net-zero: Companies shall remove carbon from the atmosphere and permanently store it to counterbalance the impact of any unabated emissions that remain once companies have achieved their long-term science-based target, and for subsequent years thereafter. The neutralization of unabated emissions applies to both the emissions reduction target(s) boundary and to any unabated emissions that have been excluded from the GHG inventory.
Target formulation
C31 - Target formulation: Companies shall publicly set a net-zero target that clearly and transparently communicates each of the target's relevant components including (a) net-zero target year, (b) magnitude of emissions reductions that will be achieved for near-term and long-term SBTs, and (c) a base year.
Reporting
C32 - Frequency: The company shall publicly report its company-wide GHG emissions inventory and progress against published targets on an annual basis.
C33 - Reporting completeness: Companies shall publicly report information pertaining to progress against validated targets, including separately reporting emissions and removals in the annual GHG inventory.
Recalculation and target validity
C34 -Mandatory target recalculation: Targets must be reviewed, and if necessary, recalculated and revalidated, at a minimum every 5 years. A company's base year emissions recalculation policy must include a significance threshold of 5% or less that is applied to emission recalculations or in the absence of a base year emissions recalculation policy, a company must agree to apply a 5% significance threshold for emission recalculations.
C35 - Triggered target recalculation: The following changes shall trigger a target recalculation:
  • Scope 3 emissions become 40% or more of aggregated scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions (applies only to near-term SBTs).
  • Emissions of exclusions in the inventory or target boundary change significantly.
  • Significant changes in company structure and activities (e.g., acquisition, divestiture, merger, insourcing or outsourcing, shifts in goods or service offerings).
  • Significant adjustments to the base year inventory, data sources or calculation methodologies, or changes in data to set targets such as growth projections (e.g., discovery of significant errors or a number of cumulative errors that are collectively significant).
  • Other significant changes to projections/assumptions used in setting the science-based targets.
